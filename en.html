Welcome to YogaVisdom.no

To all of you out there who have found me and this website; welcome.

I'm very glad you're here. It is my hope that you will be able to take something from here that will serve as a guiding light in your life.

    

I came to the yoga philosophy in an indirect way. I began to be interested in hatha yoga (physical yoga) as a way to improve my surfing, which at that time (1969) was my whole life. The benefits were immediately obvious, and I started looking into the subject deeper. By reading, I began to practice simple meditation, ate vegetarian food, and spent more and more time in deeper contemplation of the meaning of life. I had always been a traveler, but now I started on an inner journey. Along the way, my world, both the outer and the inner, became more peaceful and calm.


In 1971, I was so incredibly fortunate (not by coincidence I'm sure) to get in touch with my Spiritual Masters. Under their expertise and guidance, I received and began to follow the ancient science called Bhakti Yoga, which I have been following up until this day. And what a beautiful and fulfilling journey it has been!


So I strongly wish that you will have the same chance that I have had, undeserving as I am.


Best wishes,

Balakhilya das

Karma

5 December in Oslo

When you look at the world, many things may seem unfair. Why do bad things happen to good or innocent people?

Most of us have heard of the law of karma, which is described by familiar sayings like, “what goes around comes around,” and “as you sow, so shall you reap.” Come hear Balakhilya das, Vedic teacher and expert in the Yoga Wisdom, unravel the secret of Karma and suffering!

You will get clear and profound answers
to questions such as:
- What can I do to relieve the suffering of others?
- How is the law of karma working, and how does it affect me?
- How can I live a life without creating karma?

5 December 18:00
Litteraturhuset, Wergelandsveien 29

Lost in the World

6 December in Oslo
9 December in Trondheim

Our society is progressing faster than ever, always with new gadgets and expectation to keep up with. We are constantly available both online and offline - and still 4 out of 10 Norwegians experience loneliness. One could say “We are never alone - but we feel more lonely than ever.” And even when you are with your close family or best friends - you may still sometimes have this gnawing feeling of emptiness and loneliness deep inside of you.

Where is this feeling coming from, and what is it that we are actually longing for? Hear world-known Yoga Wisdom expert and Vedic teacher Balakhilya das speak on “Lost in the World”. This enlightening seminar will help you on the path towards a life of joy, peace, and harmony.
This may just change your life!
